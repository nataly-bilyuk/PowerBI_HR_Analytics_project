# PowerBI_HR_Analytics_project
Staff Attrition Analysis in Power BI (Atlas Labs)
This project is a Power BI case study focused on staff attrition for a fictitious software company, Atlas Labs. The goal is to build an end‑to‑end HR analytics report that helps the organization understand who is leaving, why they are leaving, and what actions can be taken to retain more employees.

Starting from a blank report, I import, model, and visualize HR data to create a set of interactive pages covering hiring trends, diversity and inclusion, performance tracking, and attrition drivers.

Project goals
Build an HR analytics report from scratch in Power BI, covering the full development lifecycle.

Explore and understand staff attrition and its key drivers across roles, tenure, demographics, and travel behavior.

Support HR decision‑making with clear, actionable insights on retention and employee wellbeing.

Demonstrate Power BI skills in data preparation, modeling, DAX, and interactive reporting.

Data and Power BI approach
Data loading and preparation:

Start from an empty Power BI report and load multiple HR datasets (employees, hiring, performance, demographics, etc.).

Rename tables and fields for clarity and verify automatically assigned data types, updating them where necessary.

Dedicated date table:

Create a date table to ensure accurate and consistent date/time reporting across all visuals and measures.

Data modeling:

Connect six tables into a coherent model using active and inactive relationships, forming a star/snowflake schema suitable for HR analytics.

Use relationships to enable cross‑filtering between hiring, performance, demographics, and attrition data.

DAX and visuals:

Use DAX measures and calculated columns to compute key HR metrics (attrition rates, hiring counts, diversity metrics, performance trends).

Key insights
Hiring and workforce overview
From all employees hired in 2021, 116 are still with Atlas Labs, giving an initial view of retention for recent cohorts.

The report surfaces age and gender distributions, including confirmation that there are 92 active female employees aged 40–49, helping HR understand representation across age brackets.

Diversity and inclusion
The analysis includes marital status and ethnicity to support Diversity & Inclusion monitoring.

Mixed race employees earn an average salary of $106,133, which is one of the lowest average salaries across all groups—a potential equity concern flagged for further investigation.

Performance tracking
A dedicated Performance Tracker page allows HR to monitor individual employees’ yearly performance scores.

For example, Estelle’s performance review was marked as “Needs Improvement”, and her satisfaction metrics show a downward trend.

This raises important questions:

Is declining satisfaction linked to performance?

How can Atlas Labs better support employees like Estelle?

The report emphasizes the need to give employees a voice and understand their context before making decisions.

Attrition analysis
The final report page focuses on employee attrition (turnover)—employees leaving the organization for any reason, voluntary or involuntary.

Sales Representatives:

Despite being a smaller job role group, they have the highest turnover.

This is a critical insight that suggests deeper investigation into workload, expectations, compensation, or support for this role.

Travel frequency and attrition:

Employees classified as frequent travelers have the highest attrition rate at 24.9%.

There is a clear trend: as travel frequency increases, attrition rises.

This suggests that travel demands may negatively impact employee happiness, making it a key area for policy review and employee feedback.

Tenure and attrition:

Employees who have worked at Atlas Labs for 5 years show an attrition rate of 17.4%.

This indicates that mid‑tenure employees may be at risk of leaving, possibly due to plateauing growth, role stagnation, or unmet expectations.

Age and longest tenure:

The oldest employee to have worked at Atlas Labs is 51 years old, providing context for age distribution and long‑term retention.

Business recommendations
Review travel policies and expectations:

Given the high attrition among frequent travelers, Atlas Labs should:

Assess whether all travel is necessary.

Offer alternatives (remote meetings, reduced travel loads).

Collect employee feedback on how travel affects work–life balance and satisfaction.

Investigate Sales Representative turnover:

Conduct targeted analysis and interviews to understand why this role has the highest attrition.

Consider adjustments to workload, support, incentives, or career paths.

Monitor mid‑tenure employees (around 5 years):

Develop career development plans, internal mobility options, and recognition programs to keep mid‑tenure employees engaged.

Address potential pay equity issues:

Review compensation for Mixed race employees, given their relatively low average salary compared to other groups.

Ensure pay structures align with role, performance, and experience rather than demographic factors.

Use performance and satisfaction data together:

For employees like Estelle, combine performance scores with satisfaction metrics to identify those at risk of leaving.

Create support pathways (coaching, workload review, role adjustments) rather than relying solely on performance ratings.

What this project demonstrates
End‑to‑end Power BI development: from blank report to a fully modeled, multi‑page HR analytics solution.

Data modeling and DAX proficiency: building relationships, dedicated date tables, and HR‑specific measures.

HR analytics and storytelling: turning raw HR data into clear, actionable insights on attrition, diversity, performance, and retention.

Business impact focus: every insight is tied to practical actions Atlas Labs can take to improve employee experience and reduce turnover.
